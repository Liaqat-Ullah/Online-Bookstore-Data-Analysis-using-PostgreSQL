# Online-Bookstore-Data-Analysis-using-PostgreSQL
This project analyzes an online bookstore database using PostgreSQL. It focuses on extracting meaningful insights from three core tables: Customers, Books, and Orders.  The project demonstrates the use of SQL to solve real-world business problems, ranging from basic data retrieval to advanced analytical queries.

**Objectives**
Understand customer purchasing behavior
Analyze book sales and performance
Evaluate revenue trends
Apply SQL to solve practical business questions
**Database Structure**

The database consists of:

Customers → Customer details (name, country, city)
Books → Book information (title, author, genre, price, stock)
Orders → Transaction data (order date, quantity, total amount)
**Tools & Technologies**
PostgreSQL
SQL (Structured Query Language)
**SQL Skills Demonstrated**
**Basic Queries (11)**
Filtering data using WHERE
Sorting with ORDER BY
Aggregations using SUM
DISTINCT values extraction
**Examples:**
Retrieve all Fiction books
Find books published after 1950
Identify customers from Canada
Calculate total stock of books
Find the most expensive and lowest stock books
Compute total revenue
**Advanced Queries (9)**
JOIN operations (Customers, Orders, Books)
GROUP BY with aggregations
HAVING clause for filtering grouped data
Subqueries and business logic
**Examples:**
Total books sold per genre
Customers with at least 2 orders
Most frequently ordered book
Top 3 most expensive Fantasy books
Total books sold by each author
Customer who spent the most
Remaining stock after fulfilling orders
**Business Questions Solved**

**This project answers key business questions such as:**

Which genres generate the most sales?
Who are the most valuable customers?
Which books are most frequently ordered?
What are the sales patterns over time?
How does inventory change after fulfilling orders?
**Key Insights**
High-value customers contribute significantly to total revenue
Certain genres (e.g., Fiction/Fantasy) dominate sales
Frequently ordered books indicate customer preferences
Inventory tracking helps identify stock shortages
Repeat customers play an important role in business growth
